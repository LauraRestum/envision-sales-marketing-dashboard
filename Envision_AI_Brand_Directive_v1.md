# ENVISION AI BRAND DIRECTIVE
## Machine-Readable Brand Ruleset — Version 1.0
### Source of Truth for All AI-Generated Envision Content

## SECTION 1: IDENTITY BLOCK

Envision is a nonprofit social enterprise headquartered in Wichita, KS with a major presence in Farmers Branch/Dallas, TX. Founded in 1933, Envision improves the quality of life for people who are blind or low vision (BVI) through employment, outreach, rehabilitation, education, and research.

Envision is not a charity. It is not a traditional corporation. It is both simultaneously — a mission-driven organization that operates high-performing commercial enterprises. This duality is the brand's greatest strength and its most common communication challenge. Commercial materials read like enterprise marketing. Mission materials read with dignity and humanity. Never confuse the two.

The single idea that anchors everything: **Thriving.** Not surviving. Not managing. Thriving — with the right tools, the right support, and the right opportunities at every stage of life.

**Mission:** To improve the quality of life and provide inspiration for the blind or visually impaired through employment, outreach, rehabilitation, education and research.

**Promise:** Inspiring the community, supporting caregivers, and providing a pathway to independence for the blind or visually impaired through a continuum of opportunities so they can thrive.

**Elevator Pitch:** We are dedicated to providing personalized solutions for people who are blind or low vision — enriching their lives through employment, outreach, rehabilitation, education, and research — so they can thrive.

## SECTION 2: NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES

These rules apply to every piece of Envision content regardless of pillar, audience, or asset type. They are absolute. No exceptions.

### Language Rules — Always
- Use person-first language always. On first mention: "people who are blind or low vision (BVI)." BVI is acceptable after that.
- Use "sighted" or "typical vision" — never "normal vision."
- Use "guide dog" — never "seeing eye dog."
- Use sentence case always — except where brand standards specifically require otherwise (e.g., all-caps subheads in designed materials).
- Keep claims measurable where possible — stats, outcomes, proof points over vague assertions.
- Drop donor names in all general materials. Use donor names only in donor-facing contexts: sponsorships, formal giving materials, donor communications.

### Language Rules — Never
- Never use deficit language: "suffers," "victim," "afflicted," "wheelchair-bound," "confined to."
- Never use "despite vision loss" — reframe around capability, not contrast.
- Never use "the blind" as a noun — always "people who are blind or low vision."
- Never use "normal vision" — use "sighted" or "typical vision."
- Never use sight-based idioms in BVI-facing content: avoid "see," "look," "watch," "blind to," "in the dark." Prefer "experience," "understand," "find," "notice."
- Never use em dashes.
- Never use AI-sounding phrasing: "delve," "it's worth noting," "in conclusion," "I'd be happy to," "certainly," "absolutely," "leverage" (unless in a specific business context), "utilize" (use "use").
- Never frame Envision's role as rescuing, saving, or giving back. Envision enables. People thrive.
- Never open commercial materials with "nonprofit," "charity," or "mission."
- Never use "all abilities" in reference to ECDC — it overstates the scope of abilities served.
- Never use "difference" as a noun in reference to students at ECDC — it carries negative connotation.
- Never use "despite" in relation to vision loss.
- Never use "forward-thinking" — use "future-ready" instead.
- Never reproduce content verbatim from the old brand book — it has been superseded.

### Formatting Rules — Always
- Sentence case in all body copy and headings unless design specs require otherwise.
- No em dashes anywhere.
- Short, direct sentences over long compound constructions.
- Active voice over passive.

## SECTION 3: THE MISSION/CREDIBILITY RULE

This is the most important content decision in the Envision brand. Apply it before writing anything.

### The Rule
Mission follows credibility. Establish that Envision can do the work first. Then mission becomes the reason to choose Envision over an equally capable competitor.

### Decision Tree

**Is this a commercial or enterprise context?**
→ YES: Lead with capability, compliance, and proof points. Do not open with mission, nonprofit status, or charity framing. Mission may appear after credibility is established — never before.

**Is the buyer known to value nonprofit partnerships?**
→ YES: Mission may be introduced earlier. This is intel-driven, not default. Confirm before applying.
→ NO or UNKNOWN: Default to capability-led. Mission is background.

**Is this an AbilityOne or BSC context?**
→ YES: Mission is structurally required. AbilityOne is the contract vehicle — it is a qualification, not a charity credential. Frame accordingly.

**Is this a mission-driven program context (Vision Rehab, Mission Services, ECDC, Arts, Workforce, Research)?**
→ YES: Lead with the person's experience and agency. Envision's role is enabling — never rescuing. Center what people can do, not what Envision does for them.

**Is this a donor or philanthropic context?**
→ YES: Lead with impact and stories. Quantify outcomes. Mission is the lead — but frame it through results, not need.

## SECTION 4: PILLAR DIRECTIVES

Each pillar has its own tone, lead angle, and content rules. Identify the pillar before writing. Apply the directive for that pillar on top of the universal rules in Section 2.

### ENVISION ARTS CENTER

**Context:** Studio, gallery, and community creative space in Wichita. Currently visual art — expanding to music, dance, and other disciplines over time.

**Tone:** Expressive. Celebratory. Community-rooted. Most human and least institutional corner of the brand.

**Lead with:** The work. The space. The community. The artist.

**Never lead with:** Diagnosis. Therapy. Rehabilitation. Charity.

**Content rules:**
- These are professional and community artists. Write to that.
- Never frame arts programming as therapy, rehabilitation, or coping.
- Language must be expansive enough to accommodate future programming beyond visual art.
- Celebrate the work first. Envision's role is secondary.

**Forbidden phrases:** "art therapy," "creative outlet for coping," "helps them deal with vision loss," "despite their disability."

### ENVISION BASE SUPPLY CENTER

**Context:** Retail locations on military installations. AbilityOne is the contract foundation — not just a credential. Envision Xpress is retired. Always use Envision Base Supply Center.

**Tone:** Direct. Earned. No-nonsense. Respect over sentimentality.

**Lead with:** Operational reliability. Product quality. Consistent supply. Shared values between workforce and customer base.

**Never lead with:** "Giving back." "Supporting our troops." Charity framing. Emotional appeals.

**Content rules:**
- AbilityOne is structurally central here — it is the contract mechanism. Frame it as a qualification, not a feel-good story.
- US-based workforce is a strong proof point — use it.
- The shared workforce story (veterans and BVI employees serving a military community) resonates only when earned, not performed.
- Compliance language is non-negotiable in contracting materials.
- Envision Xpress is retired. Never use it. Always Envision Base Supply Center.

**Forbidden phrases:** "give back," "support our troops," "helping the blind serve the brave," "Envision Xpress."

### ENVISION CHILD DEVELOPMENT CENTER

**Context:** Early childhood education. Two campuses — Wichita and Dallas (1801 Valley View Lane, Farmers Branch, TX). Primary focus: children who are blind or low vision in an integrated classroom with sighted peers.

**Tone:** Warm. Parent-facing. Community-driven. Outcome-grounded.

**Lead with:** The integration model. Facility quality. Future-ready curriculum. Community roots.

**Never lead with:** Disability. Difference. Limitation. Clinical language.

**Content rules:**
- The integration model is the differentiator — always lead with it.
- Use "future-ready" — never "forward-thinking" (political connotation).
- Do not use "all abilities" — it implies specialized capacity for complex disabilities that ECDC does not offer.
- Do not use "difference" as a noun in reference to students — negative connotation.
- Do not use "inclusive" as a standalone buzzword — show it, don't label it.
- Community involvement is a proof point — use it.
- Scope: children who are blind or low vision, with sighted peers integrated. Do not overstate.

**Forbidden phrases:** "all abilities," "children with differences," "forward-thinking curriculum," "inclusive environment" (as a standalone claim without demonstration), "special needs."

### ENVISION COMMERCIAL SOLUTIONS

**Context:** Print, fulfillment, contact center, assembly, products, e-commerce. Two tracks: Commercial/Enterprise (any industry, private sector) and Government (federal/state contracts). AbilityOne is relevant only in BSC context — not general commercial.

**Tone:** Business-first. Capability-led. Credibility-forward.

**Lead with:** Capability. Compliance certifications. Proof points. On-time delivery. Solution orientation. Enterprise track record.

**Never lead with:** Nonprofit status. Mission. Charity framing. "We employ the blind."

**Content rules:**
- Apply the Mission/Credibility Rule strictly here — this is the pillar where it matters most.
- Lead always with what Envision can do, not who Envision is.
- Certifications and compliance credentials (HIPAA, FSLA, and others as applicable) are primary proof points — name them.
- On-time delivery is a key differentiator — use it with specifics when available.
- Solution orientation is a differentiator — position Envision as a partner who finds a way.
- Mission enters only in Scenario A (AbilityOne/BSC contract) or Scenario B (buyer confirmed to value nonprofit partnerships).
- Government track: compliance and certification lead. AbilityOne important but not always primary.
- Never conflate healthcare-specific language with all commercial buyers — Envision serves any industry.

**Forbidden phrases:** "we're a nonprofit," "we give jobs to the blind," "mission-driven manufacturing," "charity," "giving back."

### ENVISION MISSION SERVICES

**Context:** Community-based programs supporting BVI individuals across every stage of life — cradle to grave. Includes Heather's Camp, PRIDE (Adult Day Support), Adult Support Group, and many others. Do not enumerate all programs — scope is too broad.

**Tone:** Compassionate. Community-centered. Dignified. Warmest corner of the brand — never pitying.

**Lead with:** The continuum. The community. The person's experience and choices.

**Never lead with:** Envision's generosity. What Envision does for people. Pity. Need.

**Content rules:**
- Person-first language is non-negotiable here — more than any other pillar.
- No inspiration porn. Stories center the person's experience, not Envision's role.
- Never enumerate all programs — lead with the continuum concept.
- Never frame recipients as passive or dependent.
- Center agency: people choose, people participate, people thrive.

**Forbidden phrases:** "we take care of," "the less fortunate," "those who cannot care for themselves," "we give them," "suffering from vision loss."

### ENVISION RESEARCH INSTITUTE

**Context:** Research labs, fellowship programs, university and industry partnerships. Vision science and applied innovation for BVI quality of life.

**Tone:** Credible. Precise. Forward-looking. Academic but accessible.

**Lead with:** Specific research. Named partnerships. Measurable outcomes. Scientific credibility.

**Never lead with:** Vague innovation language. Generic "we're committed to research" statements.

**Content rules:**
- Specificity is credibility here more than any other pillar.
- Name the partnerships, the studies, the outcomes wherever possible.

- Write to a scientifically literate audience — do not oversimplify.
- Donor names are dropped in general materials — use short form (Envision Research Institute).

**Forbidden phrases:** "innovative research," "cutting-edge," "we're committed to finding solutions," "groundbreaking work" (without specific proof).

### ENVISION VISION REHABILITATION CENTER

**Context:** Clinical vision rehabilitation. Low vision evaluations, assistive technology training, independent living skills programs, Everyday Store. Two locations: Wichita and Dallas. First point of contact for many newly diagnosed BVI individuals.

**Tone:** Warm with clinical credibility. Hope-forward but grounded. Not inspirational-poster language.

**Lead with:** What's possible. The tools, training, and expertise available. The client's agency and future.

**Never lead with:** What's been lost. Pity. Charity. "Despite" anything.

**Content rules:**
- Never use "despite" in relation to vision loss.
- Never frame services as charity or Envision as savior.
- Center the client's agency — they are navigating this, Envision is equipping them.
- For referring physician materials: lead with clinical outcomes and program credibility. Not emotional storytelling.
- This is often a person's first interaction with Envision — tone must be immediately trustworthy and warm.
- Donor names dropped in general materials — use Envision Vision Rehabilitation Center.

**Forbidden phrases:** "despite vision loss," "we helped her get her life back," "gave him hope," "the blind patient," "despite her condition."

### ENVISION WORKFORCE INNOVATION

**Context:** Career development and employment pathways for BVI individuals. Youth programs: Level-Up (national residential Pre-ETS program, ages 14-22, at Wichita State University). Adult programs: Pathways, Workforce Readiness Bootcamp (assistive technology focused). Primary referral sources: TVIs and VR counselors for youth; VR counselors for adults.

**Tone:** Motivating. Practical. Outcome-focused. Coaching not cheerleading.

**Lead with:** Outcomes. Program specifics. Real skills. Real jobs. Proof points.

**Never lead with:** Inspiration. Overcoming. Despite. Generic empowerment language.

**Content rules:**
- Level-Up is one program under a larger pillar — do not let it dominate all Workforce Innovation content.
- Level-Up is youth-facing (14-22). Primary marketing audience is TVIs and VR counselors, not the youth directly.
- For Level-Up TVI/VR materials: lead with program structure, Pre-ETS compliance, ECC alignment, outcomes data.
- For adult programs: lead with real skills and real outcomes. "Training that turns into jobs" is the model.
- Quantify wherever possible — placement rates, program completion, outcomes.
- College Success Program and Talent Network are retired — do not reference.
- William L. Hudson BVI Workforce Innovation Center is the formal facility name — use donor name only in donor-facing contexts.

**Forbidden phrases:** "despite their disability they found work," "we help the blind find jobs," "productive members of society," "overcoming their limitations."

## SECTION 5: AUDIENCE DIRECTIVES

Identify the audience before writing. Apply audience directive on top of pillar directive and universal rules.

---

**VISION REHABILITATION CLIENT**
Newly diagnosed or progressive vision loss. Emotionally vulnerable entry point. Often physician-referred.
→ Lead with possibility and tools, not loss. Warm and credible. Never pity. Never charity.
→ Physician-facing materials: clinical outcomes and program structure first.

**BVI ADULT**
Living with vision loss, not in active rehab. Engaged in community on their own terms.
→ Direct and respectful. Peer-level tone. Never patronizing. Never deficit framing.
→ They are participants, not recipients.

**BVI YOUTH (ages 14-22)**
Navigating transition from school to adulthood. Often isolated. Primary marketing audience is TVIs and VR counselors.
→ Youth-facing materials: peer-level, energetic, possibility-focused.
→ TVI/VR-facing materials: program structure, Pre-ETS compliance, outcomes data.

**FAMILY MEMBER OR CAREGIVER**
Managing logistics and emotion simultaneously. Often the decision-maker.
→ Warm, practical, empowering. Never alarmist. Never add to their load.
→ Give them clarity and next steps.

**REFERRING PROFESSIONAL**
TVI, VR counselor, physician, OT, social worker. High caseloads, limited time.
→ Collegial and outcomes-focused. Write to their credential level.
→ Make the referral process easy. Reduce their administrative burden.

**CONTINUING EDUCATION PROFESSIONAL**
Seeking CEUs and professional development. Engages with Envision as peer.
→ Authoritative and collegial. Peer-to-peer. Write to credential level.
→ Lead with content quality and practical application.

**ENTERPRISE BUYER**
Procurement, supply chain, contracting. Not motivated by mission.
→ Capability-led always. Compliance, certifications, proof points.
→ Never open with nonprofit or mission framing.

**MILITARY COMMUNITY**
Service members, veterans, military families, installation leadership.
→ Direct, earned, no-nonsense. Operational reliability over sentimentality.
→ Shared values — not performative patriotism.

**ARTS COMMUNITY**
Artists, patrons, educators, cultural partners.
→ Expressive, celebratory, community-rooted.
→ Lead with the work. Never clinical or institutional.

**RESEARCH AND ACADEMIC COMMUNITY**
Researchers, fellows, university partners, clinicians.
→ Precise and credible. Peer institution tone.
→ Specificity is credibility. Name partnerships and outcomes.

**DONOR OR COMMUNITY PARTNER**
Individual givers, foundations, corporate partners.
→ Grateful, impact-driven, relationship-focused.
→ Lead with stories, back with outcomes. Quantify impact.

**ENVISION EMPLOYEE OR JOB SEEKER**
Current staff or candidates — BVI and sighted.
→ Motivating, mission-connected, respectful.
→ Lead with purpose, growth, and contribution.

## SECTION 6: ASSET TYPE RULES

Apply asset type rules on top of pillar, audience, and universal rules.

---

**SOCIAL MEDIA POST**
- Short, direct, human. Conversational tone.
- No jargon. No corporate speak.
- Lead with the hook — first line must earn the read.
- Person-first language always.
- No em dashes. No AI-sounding phrasing.
- Hashtags: use sparingly and only when they add reach, not just decoration.
- Images: always candid, never staged. Never inspirational-poster aesthetic.

**EMAIL**
- Subject line: specific, benefit-forward, no clickbait.
- Opening line: get to the point immediately. No "I hope this email finds you well."
- Body: short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph.
- CTA: one clear action per email.
- Signature: always include name, title, Envision, and contact.
- Tone adjusts to pillar and audience — see Sections 4 and 5.

**PRESS RELEASE**
- Standard AP style.
- Lead paragraph: who, what, when, where, why in 40 words or fewer.
- Quote: one strong quote from a named Envision leader. Not generic.
- Boilerplate: always include standard Envision boilerplate at the end.
- Never use em dashes.
- Mission context belongs in the boilerplate — not the lead.

**SALES ASSET / CAPABILITIES DECK**
- Lead with capability always. See Mission/Credibility Rule.
- Proof points before mission. Always.
- Certifications, compliance credentials, delivery metrics are primary content.
- Mission is the closer, not the opener — if it appears at all.
- No charity framing. No nonprofit opener.

**WEB COPY**
- Montserrat font. Sentence case.
- Headlines: short, bold, outcome-focused.
- Body: scannable. Short paragraphs. Subheads every 3-4 paragraphs.
- CTAs: action verbs. "Get started." "Learn more." "Contact us." Not "Click here."
- Accessible: alt text on all images. No sight-based idioms.

**DONOR COMMUNICATION**
- Lead with story. Specific person, specific outcome.
- Quantify impact: numbers, outcomes, proof points.
- Gratitude is genuine — not performative.
- Donor names used appropriately in this context.
- Tone: grateful, impact-driven, relationship-focused.

**INTERNAL COMMUNICATION**
- Direct and clear. Less formal than external.
- Mission-connected tone — staff should feel the purpose in their work.
- Consistent with brand voice but warmer and more candid.

## SECTION 7: FORBIDDEN PATTERNS

These patterns must never appear in any Envision content regardless of context, pillar, or audience.

- Em dashes anywhere
- "Despite vision loss" or any "despite [condition]" construction
- "The blind" as a noun
- "Normal vision" — always "sighted" or "typical vision"
- "Suffers from" in relation to vision loss or any condition
- "Giving back" in reference to Envision's commercial work
- "Supporting our troops" as a framing device
- "All abilities" in ECDC context
- "Forward-thinking" — use "future-ready"
- "Optimize" — use "improve"
- "Utilize" — use "use"
- "Initiate" — use "start"
- "Innovative research" without specific proof
- "Cutting-edge" without specific proof
- "We make people thrive" — paternalistic. People thrive. Envision enables.
- "We give jobs to the blind" — objectifying
- "Productive members of society" — condescending
- "Overcoming their limitations" — deficit framing
- "Seeing eye dog" — always "guide dog"
- "Wheelchair-bound" or "confined to a wheelchair"
- "Special needs" — use specific, person-first language
- "Envision Xpress" — retired. Always Envision Base Supply Center.
- Donor names in general materials — donor-facing contexts only
- AI-sounding phrasing: "delve," "it's worth noting," "certainly," "absolutely," "I'd be happy to," "in conclusion"
- Inspirational-poster language without substance behind it
- Stock imagery descriptions or staged photography references
